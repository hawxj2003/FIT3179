Overweight & Obesity


Body Mass Index (BMI) is a method used to assess overweight and obesity.
It is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared (kg/m2).



Death by Risk Factors


Obesity, responsible for 37 deaths per 100,000 people, is closely linked to several other risk factors in the chart, such as high blood pressure (109 deaths), high blood sugar (53 deaths) and high cholesterol (37 deaths). These conditions are often interconnected, as obesity increases the likelihood of developing hypertension, diabetes, and heart disease. Diet-related factors like a high intake of sodium (19 deaths) and a lack of fruits and vegetables (17 and 9 deaths, respectively) further contribute to obesity and its associated health risks, emphasizing the need for comprehensive lifestyle changes to combat these interconnected issues.

Countries and Their Corresponding Region



Prevalence of Overweight and Obesity of Each Region


Western Pacific has the highest rate of overweight, followed by Europe , Eastern Mediterranean and Americas. On the other hand, Western Pacific has the highest rate of obesity, followed by Americas, Eastern Mediterranean and Europe. Thus, we can conclude that Western Pacific has the highest prevalence of overweight and obesity since 1990 to 2022.
